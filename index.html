The approach

A therapeutic model, not traditional restraint training.

Every element is designed to reduce restrictive intervention — and the harm it can cause — by getting ahead of crisis.

🤝

Therapeutic & person-centred

Protecting dignity, relationships and emotional safety — for the people you support and your staff.

🌱

Positive Behaviour Support

Proactive, PBS-led strategies that reduce challenging behaviour and the need for any restrictive intervention.

🗣

De-escalation first

Reading triggers and early warning signs, and resolving situations long before restraint is ever considered.

⚖

Lawful & accountable

Reasonable force, human rights, duty of care and professional accountability — confidently understood.

❤

Risk-aware practice

The real risks of physical intervention, including positional asphyxia, and how to avoid causing harm.

🔄

Reflection & learning

Trauma-informed, post-incident reflection and best practice that keeps your service improving.

What you'll learn

Questions

The things providers ask first.

Is the training certificated? +
Yes. Every learner who completes the two days receives a certificate on completion.
Where is it delivered? +
On-site at your service, so techniques are practised in your own environment, on dates arranged around your rota.
How long is the course? +
Two full, practical days — covering prevention and de-escalation through to safe, lawful, last-resort intervention and recording.
Is it for children's or adults' services? +
Both. The therapeutic, least-restrictive model applies across children's residential, supported accommodation, adult social care and education settings.
Does it cover the law? +
Yes — reasonable force, human rights, duty of care and professional accountability, so your team is confident and defensible.
Do we really get the Restraint Log App free? +
Yes. Full access to the Restraint Log App is included at no extra cost for everyone you train.
